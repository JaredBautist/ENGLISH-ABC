## GRAMMAR EXPLANATION - WEEK 3

### POSSESSIVE ADJECTIVES

#### Rule: What are Possessive Adjectives?

Possessive adjectives show **WHO owns or is related to something**.

```
English Question: WHOSE?
Example: "Whose book is this?" Answer: "It's MY book." / "It's HIS book."

Common Possessive Adjectives:
  I      → MY
  you    → YOUR
  he     → HIS
  she    → HER
  it     → ITS
  we     → OUR
  they   → THEIR
```

#### Structure & Examples

```
SUBJECT + POSSESSIVE ADJ + NOUN

My       +    family       = My family
His      +    brother      = His brother
Her      +    sister       = Her sister
Our      +    house        = Our house
Their    +    parents      = Their parents
```

#### Color-Coded Chart

```
👤 I           → MY        friend
👥 You         → YOUR      family
👨 He          → HIS       brother
👩 She         → HER       sister
🤖 It          → ITS       tail
👫 We          → OUR       house
👫👫 They      → THEIR     children

IMPORTANT: Possessive adjectives are ALWAYS followed by a noun!
  ✅ "My family is large."    (possessive + noun)
  ✅ "His sister is nice."    (possessive + noun)
  ❌ "My is large."           (just the possessive = WRONG)
```

#### Using Possessives in Sentences

```
Simple Statements:
  1. "I have a family. My family is large."
  2. "She has a brother. Her brother is tall."
  3. "We have parents. Our parents are kind."

With Adjectives:
  4. "Maria has a sister. Her sister is beautiful."
  5. "I have a grandmother. My grandmother is old and friendly."
  6. "They have cousins. Their cousins are funny."

With "is/are":
  7. "My mother is a doctor."
  8. "His parents are teachers."
  9. "Our family is happy."
```

#### Common Student Errors & Corrections

| Error | Why? | Correct | Explanation |
|-------|------|---------|-------------|
| "Me family is large" | Using object pronoun | **My family** is large | Always use **my**, not "me" |
| "He sister is tall" | Forgetting possessive | **His sister** is tall | "He" needs to change to **his** |
| "Her brother is my" | Incomplete sentence | **My brother** is... | Possessive needs a noun after it |
| "Their brother is he" | Wrong pronoun order | **His brother** is... | Use his (not their) for singular |
| "Your family are big" | Mixing subject/possessive | **My family** is big | Use "my" for me, "your" for you |

#### Comparison with English Pronouns

```
PERSONAL PRONOUNS (subject):
  I, you, he, she, it, we, they

POSSESSIVE ADJECTIVES (show ownership):
  my, your, his, her, its, our, their

EXAMPLE CONTRAST:
  ✅ "She is tall."                    (subject pronoun)
  ✅ "Her family is in Spain."         (possessive adjective)
  ❌ "She family is in Spain."         (wrong - mixing)
```
