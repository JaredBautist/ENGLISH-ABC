## GRAMMAR EXPLANATION - WEEK 1

### PRESENT SIMPLE OF "TO BE"

#### Rule Statement (Simple Terms)
"To be" tells us WHO someone is and WHAT they are.  
- **I am** = you talk about yourself (you = speaker)
- **You are** = the other person  
- **He is / She is** = one man or one woman
- **We are** = you + other people / my group
- **They are** = more than one person / many people

#### Color-Coded Visual Explanation

```
AFFIRMATIVE FORM:

    I          am       a teacher
  👤 (me)    [VERB]    (job)

    You        are      from Brazil
  👥 (you)   [VERB]    (place)

    He/She     is       a doctor
  👨👩 (person) [VERB]   (job)

    We         are      engineers
  👫 (group)  [VERB]    (jobs)

    They       are      from Spain
  👫👫 (many)  [VERB]    (place)

COLOR CODING:
🔵 Subject (I, you, he, she, we, they)
🟢 Verb (am, are, is)
🟡 Complement (job, place, adjective)
```

#### Example Sentences (5 Examples)

1. **I am Maria.** (name introduction)
   - "**I am**" = I talk about myself
   - "Maria" = my name

2. **You are a teacher.** (job of the other person)
   - "**You are**" = the teacher (not me, not he)
   - "a teacher" = the job

3. **He is from Japan.** (place of origin)
   - "**He is**" = one man
   - "from Japan" = where he comes from

4. **We are doctors.** (multiple people, one job)
   - "**We are**" = my group / me + others
   - "doctors" = our profession

5. **They are from England.** (multiple people, place)
   - "**They are**" = more than 2 people
   - "from England" = their country

#### Common Student Errors & Corrections

| Error | Why It Happens | Correct Form | Explanation |
|-------|----------------|--------------|-------------|
| "I is a teacher" | Confusion with he/she rule | **I am** a teacher | Always use **am** with "I" |
| "You is from Brazil" | Copying "he is" pattern | **You are** from Brazil | Always use **are** with "you" |
| "He are a doctor" | Confusing plural/singular | **He is** a doctor | Always use **is** with singular he/she |
| "They am from Spain" | Not using plural form | **They are** from Spain | Always use **are** with "they" |
| "Maria is a engineer" | Using "a" before vowels | Maria **is an engineer** | Use "**an**" before vowel sounds (a, e, i, o, u) |

#### Conjugation Table

| Subject | Affirmative | Negative | Question |
|---------|-------------|----------|----------|
| I | **I am** | I'm not | **Am I**... ? |
| You | **You are** | You aren't / You're not | **Are you**...? |
| He/She/It | **He is / She is** | He isn't / She isn't | **Is he / Is she**...? |
| We | **We are** | We aren't / We're not | **Are we**...? |
| They | **They are** | They aren't / They're not | **Are they**...? |

### ACTIVITY 5: "GRAMMAR FILL-IN-THE-BLANK: TO BE CONJUGATION" (Slide 9)

**Objective:** Practice "to be" conjugation in context

#### Instructions for Student

6. Complete all 8 sentences to unlock next slide

#### Sentences (8 examples)

| # | Sentence | Correct Answer | Dropdown Options |
|---|----------|-----------------|------------------|
| 1 | I _____ a teacher. | **am** | am / are / is |
| 2 | You _____ from Brazil. | **are** | am / are / is |
| 3 | He _____ a doctor. | **is** | am / are / is |
| 4 | She _____ an engineer. | **is** | am / are / is |
| 5 | We _____ students. | **are** | am / are / is |
| 6 | They _____ from Spain. | **are** | am / are / is |
| 7 | Maria _____ my friend. | **is** | am / are / is |
| 8 | I _____ a singer. | **am** | am / are / is |

#### Visual Design
- **Sentence layout:**
  - Top: Full sentence with blank
  - Blank shown as: [___________]
  - Below: Dropdown selector
  - Icon: "?" (click for hint)
  - Status: Red ❌ or Green ✓

#### Success Criteria
✅ Student selects correct "to be" form in 8/8 sentences  
✅ Student understands subject-verb agreement  
✅ Student can identify pronouns

#### Feedback Messages
- **Correct:** "✓ Yes! With 'I', we use **am**."
- **Incorrect - First Attempt:** "Try again. Look at the subject (I, you, he, she, we, they)."

**Anticipated Error:** Students consistently choose "is" for all subjects
- **Correction Strategy:** Highlight the subject in the sentence + show color-coded chart

**Anticipated Error:** Students confuse "are" and "is"
- **Correction Strategy:** Use visual counting (is = singular/one, are = plural/many)  
- **Cue:** "Sounds like 'izzz' = one person (he/she). Sounds like 'arr' = many people (you/we/they)."
