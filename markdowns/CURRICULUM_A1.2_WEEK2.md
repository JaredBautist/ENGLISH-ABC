## GRAMMAR EXPLANATION - WEEK 2

### PRESENT SIMPLE - AFFIRMATIVE & NEGATIVE

#### Rule 1: BASIC STRUCTURE - DO / DOES

"DO/DOES" = what you do every day (habits, routines)

**Color-Coded Structure:**

```
Subject         DO/DOES      Base Verb + (s)    Time
────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I               do           help               at 5pm
You             do           work               every day
He/She/It       does         work*              every day
We              do           sleep              at 11pm
They            do           exercise           in the morning

*Note: Add -S to verb when subject is he/she/it
```

#### Rule 2: THE "-S" ENDING FOR HE/SHE/IT

When the subject is **He / She / It**, add **-s** to the base verb:

```
✅ CORRECT:
  - She sleeps at 10pm
  - He works in the morning
  - It starts at 8:00

❌ INCORRECT:
  - She sleep at 10pm        → Should be: **sleeps**
  - He work in the morning   → Should be: **works**
  - It start at 8:00         → Should be: **starts**
```

**Pronunciation Rules for "-S":**
- After /s, z, ʃ, ʒ, tʃ, dʒ/ sounds: Add /ɪz/ → watch**es** [ˈwɑtʃɪz]
- After voiceless consonants /p, t, k, f, θ/: Add /s/ → work**s** [wɜrks]
- After voiced sounds: Add /z/ → go**es** [ɡoʊz], stud**ies** [ˈstʌdiz]

#### Rule 3: NEGATIVES - DON'T / DOESN'T

**Structure:**

```
NEGATIVE = Subject + DON'T/DOESN'T + Base Verb (NO -S!)

Subject          DON'T/DOESN'T    Verb (bare)      
───────────────────────────────────────────────
I                don't            work              (at night)
You              don't            exercise         (every day)
He/She/It        doesn't          work*             (at night)
We               don't            sleep             (in the afternoon)
They             don't            study             (on Sundays)

*Important: With doesn't, drop the -s from the verb!
  ✅ He doesn't work
  ❌ He doesn't works
```

#### Example Sentences (10 Examples)

**Affirmative:**
1. "I **wake up** at 7:00 am every day."
2. "She **watches TV** in the evening."
3. "We **have lunch** at 12:00 pm."
4. "He **goes to work** at 8:00 am."
5. "They **sleep** at 11:00 pm."

**Negative:**
6. "I **don't sleep** during the day."
7. "She **doesn't work** on Sundays."
8. "We **don't have breakfast** very early."
9. "He **doesn't exercise** in the evening."
10. "They **don't watch TV** all day."

#### Common Student Errors & Corrections

| Error | Why? | Correct | Explanation |
|-------|------|---------|-------------|
| "He go to work" | No -s ending | **He goes** | He/She needs **-s** |
| "He doesn't goes" | Double -s | **He doesn't go** | Don't/Doesn't replaces do, so no -s on verb |
| "I doesn't work" | Wrong auxiliary | **I don't work** | I always use **don't**, not doesn't |
| "She don't sleep" | Wrong auxiliary | **She doesn't sleep** | She/He/It use **doesn't**, not don't |
| "They does exercise" | Wrong subject | **They do exercise** | They use **do**, not does |
